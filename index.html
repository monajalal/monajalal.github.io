My name is Mona Jalal. I am a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate in Computer Vision at Boston University advised by Professor Vijaya Kolachalama. Currently the focus of my research is efficient deep-learning based computer vision methods for cancer detection from H&E stained digital pathology whole-slide images. Prior to joining Kolachalama's lab, I have also been contributing to projects on image and Text Analysis of Public Communications as a member of BU Artificial Intelligence for Emerging Media (AIEM) team. Prior to joining BU, I was a R&D Engineer 1 at University of California, Berkeley Center for Augmented Cognition working on an open-source framework for gesture recognition named OpenARK where I provided support for Intel RealSense SR300 camera for OpenARK augmented reality framework as well as creating a benchmark for OpenARK using CVAR hand pose dataset. I graduated from University of Wisconsin-Madison with double major Master's in computer sciences (May 2016) and electrical engineering (August 2014). I have done projects in computer vision, data analysis, database management systems, big data systems and frameworks, Internet of Things, computer architecture, and dependable and fault-tolerant embedded systems. My computer science master project topic was creating and controlling a smart home using Internet of Things. In my free time I like to learn about new software and hardware technologies and frameworks, ride my bike, and take 10k+ step walks. I did my first 100 KM bike ride on July 18, 2020, and I am lookig forward to my first 100 miles bike ride sometimes in 2022.
